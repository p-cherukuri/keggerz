What Do We Do?

Ever had trouble finding a keg of your choice on short notice? By entering your location and the type of keg you want, Keggers solves that problem for you. We hope to connect liquor store owners offering the best deals on kegs with the consumers looking for them.

At Keggers, we believe that time is your most valuable asset. In the wake of a busy schedule, no one wants to spend extra time and energy on calling multiple liquor stores to find the keg you want. We aim to make that information available to consumers before they even dial a number, and eliminate the entire calling process with the click of a button.

In today's tech-savvy society, solutions often come in the form of websites and apps. That's why Keggers uses the most reliable coding languages to maintain its website. We have proficient developers working on new ideas, and our expert technicians keep things running smoothly.

Keggers allows you to reserve kegs from our vast and trusted network of participating liquor stores. These businesses are members of the Keggers website, and for a monthly or annual subscription, consumers can easily locate them while using our website, and reserve kegs on the fly with the click of a button.

Services

The Keggers team provides three services that all relate to one another.

Keg Reservations

Finding a keg of your choice has never been easier! To reserve a keg, we don't require users to make an account. All we ask for is the user's zip code, preferred search radius, preferred keg size, manufacturer, and beer brand. We'll show you the closest stores that have it available so you can compare prices. Then simply hit the 'Reserve Now!' button, and you'll get a confirmation number to bring to the store to verify your purchase.

Users MUST be 21 years or older to complete in-store purchase of kegs.

Store Advertising

Get your store/distribution center located on our website, and watch your profits grow. Keggers makes it quick and easy for people to find kegs from stores located on our website. Just create a store owner account, and pick between a monthly or annual subscription plan. Stores located in Keggers' database will receive keg reservation numbers from Keggers users, thus seeing a boost in sales. We even offer a 3 month free trial so you can assess your profits before officially joining!

Click the link at the top to join our network and choose a subscription plan.

Inventory Management

Managing large amounts of inventory is never easy, especially with kegs. Keeping track of different sizes, manufacturers, brands, and quantity manually can get confusing. As a benefit of joining our store owner network, Keggers offers inventory management consulting services for local liquor businesses to stay organized and maximize profits by keeping track of keg quantities online. Our technicians will get you set up with a secure database on our server.

Send us an e-mail in the Contact Us section to get started. Only for store owners.

Online Beer, Wine and Liquor Sales in the US Industry Market Research Report from IBISWorld Has Been Updated

Alcohol sales will increase as the youngest Millennials reach drinking age in 2015. For these reasons, industry research firm IBISWorld has updated a report on the Online Beer, Wine and Liquor Sales industry in its growing industry report collection.

Over the past five years, the Online Beer, Wine and Liquor Sales industry has exhibited growth driven by an expansion in households with internet connections. For example, during the five years to 2014, the number of broadband connections is expected to skyrocket at an annualized rate of 19.8%, creating a robust potential customer base for the industry. Furthermore, according to Nielsen's 2014 E-commerce report, 10.0% of consumers in North America browsed and purchased alcoholic beverages online over a six-month period, which bolstered industry revenue. “The increased number of time-strapped, amateur wine connoisseurs and individuals interested in mixology and craft beer has also raised the popularity of online alcohol purchases,” according to IBISWorld Analyst Sarah Turk.
